Discounting Without Killing Profit

Discounts can increase orders fast — but they can also destroy margins quietly. Here’s how to discount safely like a smart business owner.

Most businesses use discounts for one reason: to get more sales. But the real goal should be: more profit, not just more orders.

Step 1: Know your real profit per order

Before running any offer, you should know how much you earn from 1 order after costs. Your “true cost” is usually higher than you think.

True Cost = Product cost + packaging + delivery + platform fee + payment fee + returns risk

If you discount without knowing this, you might sell more and still lose money.

Why discounts hurt more than you think

Discounts reduce your selling price, but most costs stay the same. That means your profit drops faster than the discount percentage.

⚠️ Fact: A 20% discount can reduce your actual net profit by 50%+ depending on your margin structure.

Simple example

Suppose you have the following unit economics:

  • Selling price = ₹1,000
  • True cost = ₹750
  • Profit = ₹250

Now you give a 20% discount:

  • New selling price = ₹800
  • True cost stays = ₹750
  • New profit = ₹50
A 20% Discount → Profit drops from ₹250 to ₹50 (An 80% drop in profit!)

When discounts make sense

Discounts are not always bad. They are highly effective when used strategically:

  • Clearing old stock (End of season, slow-moving items)
  • Increasing repeat purchase (Coupon for returning customers)
  • Increasing AOV (Discount above a certain cart value)
  • Launching a new product (Small first-week introductory offer)

Smarter discount types (Better than flat % off)

Offer Type Example Why it’s safer
Threshold discount ₹100 off above ₹999 Forces an increase in cart value
Bundle offer Buy 2, save 10% Yields a higher profit mass per order
Free shipping Free delivery above ₹799 Feels highly valuable, reduces discount pressure
Gift with purchase Free sample above ₹999 Higher perceived value, much lower cost to you

When you should avoid discounts entirely

  • Your margin is already low
  • Your delivery cost is unusually high
  • You have frequent returns/refunds in that category
  • You’re discounting only because competitors are doing it
  • You are using discounts to “force sales” instead of improving your product's perceived value

Quick rule to stay safe

Before running an offer, always ask yourself:

After the discount, will my profit per order still be healthy?

If the profit becomes too small, you’ll just get busy—you won't grow.

Discounting Without Killing Profit

Discounts can increase orders fast — but they can also destroy margins quietly. Here’s how to discount safely like a smart business owner.

Most businesses use discounts for one reason: to get more sales. But the real goal should be: more profit, not just more orders.

Step 1: Know your real profit per order

Before running any offer, you should know how much you earn from 1 order after costs. Your “true cost” is usually higher than you think.

True Cost = Product cost + packaging + delivery + platform fee + payment fee + returns risk

If you discount without knowing this, you might sell more and still lose money.

Why discounts hurt more than you think

Discounts reduce your selling price, but most costs stay the same. That means your profit drops faster than the discount percentage.

A 20% discount can reduce your profit by 50%+ (depending on your margin).

Simple example

Suppose:

  • Selling price = ₹1,000
  • True cost = ₹750
  • Profit = ₹250

Now you give a 20% discount:

  • New selling price = ₹800
  • True cost stays = ₹750
  • New profit = ₹50
Discount = 20% → Profit drops from ₹250 to ₹50 (80% profit drop)

When discounts make sense

Discounts are not always bad. They are useful when used for a clear reason:

  • Clearing old stock (end of season, slow-moving items)
  • Increasing repeat purchase (coupon for returning customers)
  • Increasing AOV (discount above a cart value)
  • Launching a new product (small first-week offer)

Smarter discount types (better than flat % off)

Offer Type Example Why it’s safer
Threshold discount ₹100 off above ₹999 Increases cart value
Bundle offer Buy 2, save 10% Higher profit per order
Free shipping Free delivery above ₹799 Feels valuable, reduces discount pressure
Gift with purchase Free sample above ₹999 Higher perceived value, less margin loss

When you should avoid discounts

  • Your margin is already low
  • Your delivery cost is high
  • You have frequent returns/refunds
  • You’re discounting only because competitors do it
  • You are using discounts to “force sales” instead of improving product value

Quick rule to stay safe

Before running an offer, ask:

After discount, will my profit per order still be healthy?

If profit becomes too small, you’ll get busy but won’t grow.

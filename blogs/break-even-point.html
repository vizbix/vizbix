Break-even Point: The Only Number You Need Early

If you’re a small business owner, break-even is the fastest way to know whether you’re safe or struggling.

Many businesses don’t fail because they don’t sell. They fail because they don’t sell enough to cover fixed costs. That’s why break-even is one of the most important numbers to track early.

What is break-even point?

Break-even means: the sales level where your business makes zero profit and zero loss. You have covered all costs. Anything above that is profit.

Break-even Sales = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution Margin %

Don’t worry about the formula name. Just remember: break-even depends on two things:

  • Fixed costs (rent, salaries, internet, software, EMIs)
  • Profit per sale after variable costs (material, shipping, packaging, platform fees)

Simple example (small business)

Let’s say you sell a product for ₹1,000.

  • Product cost + shipping + packaging = ₹650
  • So profit per order before fixed expenses = ₹1,000 − ₹650 = ₹350
  • Your fixed monthly expenses = ₹35,000
Break-even orders = ₹35,000 ÷ ₹350 = 100 orders/month

That means if you sell less than 100 orders in a month, you lose money. If you sell more than 100, you start making profit.

Why break-even is powerful

Break-even helps you answer questions like:

  • “How many orders do I need this month to survive?”
  • “Can I afford to hire someone?”
  • “Is my discount hurting me?”
  • “Should I run ads or wait?”

3 ways to reduce break-even quickly

1) Reduce fixed costs (even temporarily)

  • Pause subscriptions you don’t use
  • Negotiate rent / workspace
  • Move non-critical work to freelancers

2) Increase profit per order

  • Reduce delivery cost per order (better courier plan)
  • Stop discounts on low-margin products
  • Increase average order value using bundles

3) Sell more of your highest-margin products

  • Push the “best margin” items on homepage and ads
  • Make the profitable product your hero product
  • Upsell add-ons at checkout
Quick warning: Many owners calculate break-even using revenue only. Always calculate it using PROFIT per order (not just sales).

Profit Margin Explained (Simple)

Understand gross vs net margin and the fastest way to improve margins without raising prices.

If your sales are increasing but your bank balance is not, the problem is usually profit margin. Profit margin tells you how much money you actually keep after costs.

What is Profit Margin?

Profit margin is the percentage of revenue that becomes profit. In simple words: for every ₹100 you sell, how much is left after costs?

Profit Margin (%) = (Profit ÷ Sales) × 100

Gross Margin vs Net Margin (Don’t mix these)

Most small businesses calculate margin incorrectly because they forget there are two types:

  • Gross Margin: Profit after product cost (material, packaging, manufacturing).
  • Net Margin: Profit after all expenses (rent, salaries, ads, shipping, software).

Example

You sell a product for ₹1,000. Your product cost is ₹600. Your other monthly expenses are ₹20,000.

  • Gross profit per order = ₹1,000 − ₹600 = ₹400
  • If you sell 100 orders → gross profit = ₹40,000
  • Net profit = ₹40,000 − ₹20,000 = ₹20,000

Fastest ways to improve profit margin (without raising prices)

You don’t always need a price increase. You can improve margin by fixing operational leakage:

  • Reduce delivery/packaging cost per order
  • Stop discounts on already low-margin products
  • Bundle products to increase average order value
  • Negotiate supplier rates after achieving consistent volume
  • Remove products that look “popular” but earn zero net profit

One simple rule

If you don’t know your margin, you can’t confidently spend on ads, discounts, or hiring. Margin is the baseline of every business decision.

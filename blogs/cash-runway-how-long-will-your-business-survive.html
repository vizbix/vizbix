Cash Runway: How Long Will Your Business Survive?

Cash runway tells you how long your business can operate before money runs out. It’s one of the most important numbers for stability.

Many businesses don’t die because they are “bad”. They die because cash finishes at the wrong time. Cash runway helps you avoid surprises and plan smarter.

What is cash runway?

Cash runway means: how many months you can survive with your current cash if income stays the same (or drops).

Cash Runway (months) = Cash Available ÷ Monthly Burn

What is monthly burn?

Monthly burn is the amount of cash you lose every month. If you are profitable, burn may be zero or negative (good).

Monthly Burn = Monthly Expenses − Monthly Profit (or cash inflow)

If you don’t want complex calculations, use a simpler method:

Burn = Fixed expenses + average variable expenses (if sales drop)

Simple example

Suppose you have:

  • Cash in bank = ₹3,00,000
  • Monthly expenses (rent + salary + tools + misc) = ₹1,20,000
  • Monthly profit = ₹40,000

Monthly burn = 1,20,000 − 40,000 = ₹80,000

Runway = ₹3,00,000 ÷ ₹80,000 = 3.75 months

How to read your runway

Runway Meaning What to do
0–2 months Danger zone Cut costs immediately + focus on fast cash
2–4 months Risky Reduce burn + increase contribution margin
4–8 months Stable Improve profit + build reserve
8+ months Strong Invest carefully in growth
A profitable business can still have low runway if money is stuck in inventory or receivables. Cash runway is about cash-in-hand, not just profit.

Why small businesses run out of cash (common reasons)

  • Too much inventory sitting unsold
  • Customers paying late (credit sales)
  • High marketing spend without profit tracking
  • Large fixed costs too early (office, staff, subscriptions)
  • Discounting too aggressively

How to increase runway fast (practical)

1) Reduce burn

  • Pause non-essential tools/subscriptions
  • Renegotiate rent or move to lower-cost setup
  • Convert fixed costs to variable where possible

2) Improve contribution margin

  • Increase price slightly on best-selling products
  • Reduce shipping cost per order
  • Stop discounting low-margin items

3) Get cash faster

  • Push prepaid orders
  • Offer small prepaid discount instead of large sale discount
  • Sell slow inventory with bundles
If your runway is under 2 months, don’t “wait for next month”. Take action immediately: reduce burn + focus on cash generating products.
